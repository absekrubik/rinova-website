Overview of IELTS

The IELTS (International English Language Testing System) is a standardized English language test for non-native speakers. It is used for study, work, and migration purposes in countries like the UK, Canada, Australia, and more. IELTS assesses your skills in listening, reading, writing, and speaking through a structured test format. There are two versions: Academic, for higher education or professional registration, and General Training, for work or immigration. Scores are given on a band scale from 0 to 9, with most institutions requiring a band of 6.0 or higher.

Types of IELTS Tests

  • IELTS Academic: For those applying for higher education or professional registration. Focuses on academic English.
  • IELTS General Training: For migration, work experience, or training programs. Focuses on everyday English skills.

Test Format

The test has 4 sections, and the total time is about 2 hours 45 minutes:

  • Listening: Lasts about 30 minutes, includes 4 recordings with 40 questions. Tests understanding of spoken English in diverse contexts.
  • Reading: 60 minutes, includes 3 passages with 40 questions; varies slightly between Academic and General Training versions.
  • Writing: Two tasks: Task 1 (graph/chart or letter), Task 2 (essay). Assesses clarity and organization of ideas.
  • Speaking: 11–14 minutes face-to-face interview with 3 parts: introduction, short speech, and discussion.

Overview of Band Score in IELTS

  • Band 9 (Expert User): Full command of the language with no mistakes.
  • Band 8 (Very Good User): Strong command with occasional errors in unfamiliar situations.
  • Band 7 (Good User): Handles complex language well, understands detailed reasoning.
  • Band 6 (Competent User): Generally good command; some misunderstandings in unfamiliar situations.
  • Band 5 (Modest User): Understands main points but struggles with complex language.
  • Band 4 (Limited User): Communicates only in familiar contexts; frequent misunderstandings.
  • Band 3 (Extremely Limited User): Can convey only basic information in familiar contexts.
  • Band 2 (Intermittent User): Understands isolated words or phrases.
  • Band 1 (Non-User): Only a few isolated words understood.
  • Band 0: Did not attempt the test.
